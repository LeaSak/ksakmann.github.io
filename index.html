About

As a physicist I am working on understanding quantum dynamics of many interacting particles. I am generally interested in extracting information from available data.The physical questions that I find most interesting are the emergence of collective phenomena that occur for instance when a Bose-Einstein condensate is measured by shining a beam of light onto it. This measurement process makes all quantum correlations that are present in the condensate apparent in a simple photograph. The reconstruction of the quantum state from the experimental data is an inverse problem that requires knowledge of the correlation functions of the system. My experience in this field is predominantly theoretical with a strong background in developing new large-scale computational methods for the solution of the time-dependent many-body Schr√∂dinger equation and the evaluation of high-order correlation functions from its solution.
